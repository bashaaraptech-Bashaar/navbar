PEPSI

Pepsi is a globally iconic carbonated soft drink created in 1893 by pharmacist Caleb Bradham. Originally marketed as "Brad's Drink," it was renamed Pepsi-Cola in 1898 for its digestive properties. As the flagship brand of PepsiCo, it is famous for its historic "Cola Wars" rivalry with Coca-Cola and its youth-centric marketing. In 2026, the brand continues to lead the beverage market with its bold new visual identity and an expanded focus on low-sugar options like Pepsi Zero Sugar.

The Refreshing Story of Pepsi

More than a drink — a global culture.

Pepsi was invented in 1893 by North Carolina pharmacist Caleb Bradham as "Brad’s Drink" before being renamed Pepsi-Cola in 1898 to emphasize its digestive benefits. After surviving bankruptcies in the 1920s and 1930s, the brand surged during the Great Depression by offering 12-ounce bottles for just five cents—double the size of competitors for the same price. In 1965, a landmark merger with Frito-Lay created PepsiCo, setting the stage for decades of "Cola Wars" marked by high-profile celebrity ads and the famous Pepsi Challenge. In 2026, Pepsi continues to evolve its legacy with a focus on functional health through the launch of Pepsi Prebiotic Cola.

Where Pepsi is loacated at?

PepsiCo's global headquarters is located in Purchase, New York, at 700 Anderson Hill Road. The 112-acre campus, established in 1970, features a world-renowned architectural complex designed by Edward Durell Stone and includes the Donald M. Kendall Sculpture Gardens, which houses 45 contemporary sculptures by artists such as Alexander Calder and Henry Moore.
